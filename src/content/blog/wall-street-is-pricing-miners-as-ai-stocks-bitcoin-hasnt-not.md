## The Infrastructure Bet

Miners own power. That is the hidden asset. It is suddenly the most sought-after resource in tech. Core Scientific and Hut 8 have already shown this playbook works. Wall Street is finally baking it into valuations. The jump in [Bitcoin mining stocks](https://cointelegraph.com/news/bitcoin-mining-stocks-jump-as-ai-infrastructure-boom-boosts-sector-outlook) this week reflects a market repricing the sector not on hash price, but on power access. Riot Platforms, Marathon Digital, and Cleanspark are all trading higher. The sector's beta to the NASDAQ is rising. This is a structural shift, not a quarter-end rotation.

## The BTC Reality Check

But don't conflate the equity rally with the spot asset. [Bitcoin drops after $78K pop](https://cointelegraph.com/markets/bitcoin-drops-after-78k-pop-but-value-investor-keeps-hoovering-up-cheap-btc), and bears still hold the resistance line. The 'value investor' profile cited in the report is scooping up coins. Accumulation addresses are growing. That is a long-term bullish signal, but it means nothing for momentum today. Institutional ETF flows are fickle. Without a macro catalyst — a clear FOMC pivot or a massive inflow day — the spot market lacks the conviction to hold **$78K**. The range feels tighter, which usually precedes a breakout, but the direction is an open question. The AI mania is doing nothing for spot BTC directly. It is an indirect structural bull case for miner treasuries, not an immediate price catalyst.

The next CPI and FOMC minutes are the closest official catalysts on the calendar. Until then, the market waits.
