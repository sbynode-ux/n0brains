## The institutional rotation

Jane Street’s Q1 13F filing reveals a relative-value trade, not a directional bet. It slashed Bitcoin ETF exposure and added Ether funds. [EToro’s earnings](https://cointelegraph.com/news/etoros-net-income-jumps-37-on-commodities-surge-crypto-trades-drop-32) confirm the macro pattern: net income jumped **37%** on commodities gains while crypto trading volume dropped **32%**. Traditional shops treat digital assets as just another sector in a diversified book. They aren't all-in or all-out. They manage spreads, durations, and narratives. A **$49B** rebalancing signal doesn't vanish overnight. It ripples through order books for months. The real edge isn't reading the filing after the fact. It's anticipating the next rotation based on live data — funding rates, open interest shifts, and stablecoin flows.

## The agent infrastructure

The more important structural shift is happening quietly in the background. [x402](https://cointelegraph.com/news/coinbase-launches-x402-batch-settlement-ai-payments) now lets AI agents batch settle payments autonomously. An agent can pay for a WebSocket data feed, execute a split-second arbitrage, and settle the network fees — all without a human approving the transaction. Before this, agents were brains without wallets. They generated analysis but couldn't act on it independently. Batch settlement makes agent swarms economically viable. A fleet of bots can share datasets, split computational costs, and settle as a single entity. This is the infrastructure layer that allows autonomous strategies to run end-to-end. The agent economy just got a bank account that speaks its native protocol.

It signals a broadening of conviction and a mature capital allocation process. Money isn't leaving crypto. It's getting smarter about where it sits.
